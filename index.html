About Me

I started web design in Las Vegas while I was a teen, creating websites for my band, Ashbury. Early on, I held the belief that if we had a great logo, website, t-shirt designs, CD covers, and flyers, people would give us a chance, and I was right. As Ashbury grew better, so did my designs, and that led to amazing opportunities for us that culminated in winning a bi-national (Canada and America) competition put on by 80s rockers Bon Jovi. We played in front of approximately 60,000 people in Giant's Stadium. Later, a close friend and I crafted a viral marketing campaign that was directed at Pete Wentz and his band Fallout Boy. Using design and cleverly crafted messaging, we were able to reach the masses, and MTV wrote a story on our work.

I've since gone to school and moved into the world of full-time software development, but those early experiences still influence my work. I have a passion for helping companies of all sizes take their brand to the next level, creating new and exciting opportunities, and increasing their value. I've built large mobile apps and mobile games, and I specialize in custom web design.
